# Market Regime Indicators — Detailed Reference

## VIX (CBOE Volatility Index)

The VIX measures expected 30-day volatility of the S&P 500, derived from options prices. It's often called the "fear gauge."

### Interpretation for Regime Detection

| VIX Level | Regime Signal | What's Happening |
|-----------|--------------|------------------|
| < 14 | Strong risk-on (but watch for complacency) | Market is very calm, growth multiples tend to be at highs |
| 14-18 | Risk-on | Normal low-volatility environment, favorable for growth |
| 18-22 | Transitional / Mild concern | Some nervousness, watch the direction |
| 22-28 | Risk-off | Meaningful fear, growth stocks under pressure |
| 28-35 | Strong risk-off | High fear, growth stocks getting hammered |
| > 35 | Panic | Crisis-level fear, but also where contrarian re-entry opportunities form |

### VIX Direction Matters More Than Level

A VIX at 20 and falling is very different from a VIX at 20 and rising:
- **VIX falling** = fear receding = favorable for growth stock multiples
- **VIX rising** = fear increasing = multiples likely to compress
- **VIX spike followed by rapid decline** = classic "buy the dip" pattern for growth stocks

### VIX Term Structure

- **Contango** (front month < back months): Normal, risk-on. Market expects current calm to continue.
- **Backwardation** (front month > back months): Risk-off. Market is paying more for near-term protection — expects trouble soon.

The shift from contango to backwardation is one of the earliest risk-off signals.

## Growth vs Value Performance

### Why This Matters

Growth stocks command higher multiples because investors are paying for future earnings potential. When risk appetite declines, investors become less willing to pay for uncertain future earnings and rotate into value stocks with more certain, current cash flows.

### How to Track

Compare these pairs:
- **QQQ vs SPY**: Nasdaq-100 (growth-heavy) vs S&P 500 (broader)
- **IWF vs IWD**: iShares Growth ETF vs iShares Value ETF
- **ARKK vs SPY**: ARK Innovation (high-growth) vs S&P 500 — more extreme signal

### Reading the Signal

| Pattern | Duration | Regime Signal |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Growth outperforming by 2%+ per week | 2+ weeks | Strong risk-on |
| Growth slightly outperforming | Ongoing | Risk-on |
| Growth and Value roughly even | Recently shifted | Transitional — watch closely |
| Value outperforming by 1-2% per week | 1-2 weeks | Early risk-off |
| Value outperforming by 3%+ per week | 2+ weeks | Strong risk-off |

## Credit Spreads

### What They Tell You

Credit spreads measure the extra yield investors demand for holding riskier corporate bonds vs safe government bonds. When spreads widen, it means investors are demanding more compensation for risk — a direct measure of risk appetite.

### Key Spreads to Watch

- **High Yield Spread (HYG vs LQD or vs Treasuries)**: Most sensitive to risk appetite
- **Investment Grade Spread**: Slower moving but confirms broader trends
- **ICE BofA US High Yield Index OAS**: The institutional standard

### Interpretation

| Spread Movement | Speed | Signal |
|-----------------|-------|--------|
| Tightening (spreads getting smaller) | Gradual | Risk-on, healthy |
| Tightening | Rapid | Strong risk-on, possibly late-cycle |
| Stable | — | Neutral, current regime likely to continue |
| Widening (spreads getting larger) | Gradual | Early risk-off warning |
| Widening | Rapid (50bps+ in a week) | Strong risk-off, exit growth immediately |

Credit spreads often lead equity markets by a few days. A widening in credit before equities sell off is a valuable early warning.

## Small Cap vs Large Cap

### The Logic

Small cap stocks (Russell 2000 / IWM) are more sensitive to economic conditions and have less liquidity. When investors are confident, they're willing to own smaller, riskier companies. When fear rises, they flee to the relative safety and liquidity of large caps.

### Interpretation

- **IWM outperforming SPY**: Risk-on — investors are reaching for return
- **IWM underperforming SPY**: Risk-off — investors are retreating to safety
- **IWM breaking below its 50-day MA while SPY holds**: Early risk-off signal
- **IWM breaking above 200-day MA**: Potential regime shift to risk-on

## Market Breadth

### What It Measures

Market breadth tells you whether the market's move is supported by many stocks or just a few leaders. Broad participation is healthy and supports growth multiples. Narrow leadership is a warning sign.

### Key Metrics

- **NYSE Advance/Decline Line**: Rising = broad participation, falling = narrowing
- **% of stocks above 50-day MA**: Above 60% is healthy, below 40% is risk-off
- **% of stocks above 200-day MA**: Above 55% is healthy, below 40% is risk-off
- **New Highs vs New Lows**: More new highs = risk-on, more new lows = risk-off

### How Breadth Deterioration Typically Unfolds

1. Market makes new high but fewer stocks participate (divergence)
2. % above 50-day MA starts declining while index is flat or up
3. Advance/decline line turns down
4. Eventually the index follows

This sequence can play out over weeks. When you see step 1-2, it's an early warning to tighten stops on growth positions.

## Putting It All Together

No single indicator is reliable enough on its own. The power is in convergence:

**High Confidence Risk-On (3+ indicators agree):**
- VIX low/falling + Growth outperforming + Credit tight + Breadth broad
- This is when you want maximum exposure to high-growth stocks

**High Confidence Risk-Off (3+ indicators agree):**
- VIX high/rising + Value outperforming + Credit widening + Breadth narrow
- Exit growth completely, rotate to defensive

**Mixed Signals (indicators disagree):**
- Reduce position size, tighten stops, wait for clarity
- Don't force a directional bet when the regime is unclear
- The transitional period is where most whipsaw losses happen

**Regime Transition Detection:**
- The most valuable signal is when multiple indicators start flipping in the same direction over 3-5 trading days
- You don't need all of them to flip — 2-3 shifting together is enough to act on
- The first indicator to flip is often credit spreads or VIX term structure
