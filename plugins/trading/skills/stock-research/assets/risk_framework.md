# Risk Assessment Framework

## Categories of Investment Risk

### 1. Business/Operational Risks

**Revenue Concentration Risk**
- Customer concentration (% from top customers)
- Product concentration (single product dominance)
- Geographic concentration (over-reliance on one region)
- **Red Flag**: >20% revenue from single customer or >50% from single product

**Competitive Risks**
- Market share erosion
- New entrant threats
- Price competition intensifying
- Technological disruption
- Product obsolescence

**Execution Risks**
- Dependence on key personnel
- Complex operational model
- Integration challenges (post-acquisition)
- Supply chain vulnerabilities
- Quality control issues

**Growth Risks**
- Unsustainable growth rates
- Growth requiring heavy capital investment
- Market saturation approaching
- Difficulty scaling operations

### 2. Financial Risks

**Liquidity Risk**
- Low cash reserves relative to obligations
- Poor working capital management
- Difficulty accessing credit markets
- **Warning Signs**: Current ratio <1.0, declining cash balance

**Leverage Risk**
- High debt levels (Debt/EBITDA >4x)
- Debt maturing near-term
- Covenant violations possible
- Rising interest expense
- **Critical**: Check debt maturity schedule

**Profitability Risk**
- Declining margins
- Path to profitability unclear (for growth companies)
- Negative free cash flow
- Burn rate concerns
- **Red Flag**: Gross margin declining for 3+ consecutive quarters

**Accounting Quality**
- Aggressive revenue recognition
- Frequent one-time charges
- Large gap between earnings and cash flow
- Related party transactions
- Auditor changes or qualifications
- Complex accounting structure

### 3. Market/Industry Risks

**Cyclical Risk**
- Industry tied to economic cycles
- Commodity price exposure
- Housing or auto sales dependence
- **Assessment**: Review performance during last recession

**Structural Change Risk**
- Industry in secular decline
- Business model disruption (e.g., streaming vs. cable)
- Technology shifts (e.g., EVs vs. ICE vehicles)
- Changing consumer preferences

**Regulatory Risk**
- Pending legislation impact
- Regulatory investigations
- Compliance costs rising
- International regulatory differences
- Environmental regulations

**Market Dynamics**
- Low barriers to entry
- Commoditization trends
- Weak pricing power
- Supplier concentration

### 4. Macroeconomic Risks

**Interest Rate Sensitivity**
- High debt = vulnerable to rate increases
- Duration of debt
- Refinancing needs
- Business model sensitivity (e.g., REITs, utilities)

**Economic Sensitivity**
- Discretionary vs. essential products
- B2B vs. B2C exposure
- Performance during recessions
- Leading vs. lagging economic indicators

**Currency Risk**
- International revenue exposure
- Translation vs. transaction risk
- Hedging policies
- **Consider**: % revenue from foreign operations

**Inflation Risk**
- Ability to pass on cost increases
- Input cost volatility
- Wage pressure
- Long-term contracts at fixed prices

### 5. ESG and Governance Risks

**Environmental Risks**
- Carbon footprint and climate impact
- Regulatory compliance costs
- Stranded asset risk
- Water/resource usage

**Social Risks**
- Labor practices and workforce issues
- Product safety concerns
- Data privacy and security
- Community relations

**Governance Risks**
- Board independence and quality
- Executive compensation alignment
- Related party transactions
- Dual-class share structures
- History of shareholder-unfriendly actions
- **Red Flags**: CEO/Chairman same person, family control, low insider ownership

### 6. Company-Specific Risks

**Management Quality**
- Track record of execution
- Capital allocation discipline
- Communication transparency
- Insider selling patterns
- Management turnover
- **Warning**: Frequent C-suite changes

**Strategic Risks**
- Unclear strategy or frequent pivots
- Major acquisitions (integration risk)
- Expansion into unfamiliar markets
- Heavy R&D with uncertain payoff

**Legal and Regulatory**
- Ongoing litigation
- Patent disputes
- Regulatory investigations
- Compliance violations
- **Check**: Legal liabilities disclosed in 10-K

**Reputational Risk**
- Brand damage incidents
- Product recalls
- Customer data breaches
- Social media controversies

### Risk Mitigation Factors
- Strong balance sheet
- Diversified revenue streams
- Proven management team
- Sustainable competitive advantages
- Consistent free cash flow generation
- Conservative accounting practices

## Investment Decision Framework

**Risk-Adjusted Return Considerations**:
- Higher risk requires higher potential return
- Compare risk level to valuation discount
- Consider portfolio diversification impact
- Assess risk vs. your investment timeline

**Position Sizing Based on Risk**:
- Low Risk: Can be larger portfolio position (5-10%)
- Medium Risk: Moderate position (3-5%)
- High Risk: Small position (1-3%) or avoid

**Risk Monitoring Checklist**:
- Quarterly earnings quality
- Debt level trends
- Management commentary changes
- Competitive landscape shifts
- Regulatory developments
- Insider trading activity
- Analyst downgrades

## Red Flags Summary

**Immediate Disqualifiers** (Consider avoiding):
- Accounting fraud or material weaknesses
- Impending bankruptcy risk
- Serial dilution of shareholders
- Consistently negative free cash flow with no path to profitability
- Management with history of value destruction
- Deteriorating business with no turnaround plan

**Serious Concerns** (Proceed with extreme caution):
- Debt/EBITDA >5x
- Declining revenue for 3+ quarters
- Market share losses to competitors
- Gross margin compression >500 bps
- Major customer or supplier dependencies
- Pending adverse regulatory actions
- Industry in structural decline
