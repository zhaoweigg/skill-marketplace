# High-Growth Stock Watchlist

These are the types of stocks this strategy is designed for. They share key characteristics: high revenue growth, high beta, valuation driven primarily by market-assigned multiples, and outsized sensitivity to risk appetite shifts.

This list is a starting point. The user may have their own preferred names. What matters is that the stock fits the profile, not that it's on this list.

## Characteristics of an Ideal Stock for This Strategy

1. **Revenue growth 20%+ YoY** — the faster the better, because high growth justifies the multiple expansion that drives swing gains
2. **High beta (1.5+)** — moves more than the market, amplifying regime shifts
3. **Valuation driven by P/S more than P/E** — may not be consistently profitable yet, so the multiple on sales is what investors watch
4. **Large retail following** — retail sentiment amplifies moves in both directions
5. **Optionality in the business model** — TAM expansion story that justifies paying up when market is generous

## How to Use This List

This is a reference for the types of stocks this strategy applies to. When analyzing a specific stock:

1. Confirm it fits the profile (high growth, high beta, multiple-driven)
2. If it doesn't fit, the analysis can still be useful but the regime sensitivity assumptions may be weaker
3. The user's conviction and familiarity with the stock matters — this strategy works best with stocks the trader understands well enough to hold through volatility

## Stocks This Strategy Does NOT Work Well For

- **Blue chip / Mega cap** (AAPL, MSFT, GOOG) — too stable, multiples don't swing enough
- **Dividend stocks** — different investor base, regime-insensitive
- **Deep value / Turnaround** — driven by specific catalysts, not regime
- **Penny stocks / Pre-revenue** — too speculative, no multiple framework applies
- **Commodity stocks** — driven by commodity prices, not growth multiples
