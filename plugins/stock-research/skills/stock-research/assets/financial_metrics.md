# Financial Metrics Reference

## Key Financial Health Indicators

### Profitability Metrics
- **Gross Margin**: (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100%
  - Indicates pricing power and production efficiency
  - Compare to industry averages and competitors
  - Look for trends: improving margins = competitive strength

- **Operating Margin**: Operating Income / Revenue × 100%
  - Shows operational efficiency
  - >15% is generally strong, but varies by industry

- **Net Profit Margin**: Net Income / Revenue × 100%
  - Overall profitability after all expenses
  - Compare to peers and historical trends

- **Return on Equity (ROE)**: Net Income / Shareholder Equity × 100%
  - >15% is generally considered good
  - Measures how efficiently company uses shareholder capital

- **Return on Assets (ROA)**: Net Income / Total Assets × 100%
  - Indicates how efficiently assets generate profit
  - Compare to industry benchmarks

### Growth Metrics
- **Revenue Growth (YoY)**: (Current Revenue - Prior Revenue) / Prior Revenue × 100%
  - Look at 1-year, 3-year, and 5-year trends
  - Consistent growth is better than volatile spikes

- **Earnings Growth (YoY)**: Track EPS growth over time
  - Should ideally match or exceed revenue growth

- **Free Cash Flow Growth**: Indicates ability to generate cash for dividends, buybacks, or reinvestment

### Liquidity Metrics
- **Current Ratio**: Current Assets / Current Liabilities
  - >1.5 indicates good short-term financial health
  - <1.0 may signal liquidity concerns

- **Quick Ratio**: (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
  - More conservative than current ratio
  - >1.0 is healthy

- **Cash Ratio**: Cash / Current Liabilities
  - Most conservative liquidity measure

### Leverage Metrics
- **Debt-to-Equity Ratio**: Total Debt / Total Equity
  - <1.0 is generally conservative
  - >2.0 may indicate high financial risk
  - Compare to industry norms (utilities naturally have higher ratios)

- **Interest Coverage Ratio**: EBITDA / Interest Expense
  - >3.0 indicates comfortable debt servicing
  - <1.5 signals potential distress

- **Debt-to-EBITDA**: Total Debt / EBITDA
  - <3.0 is generally manageable
  - >5.0 may be concerning

### Efficiency Metrics
- **Asset Turnover**: Revenue / Total Assets
  - Measures how efficiently assets generate revenue

- **Inventory Turnover**: COGS / Average Inventory
  - Higher is better (faster inventory movement)

- **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)**: (Accounts Receivable / Revenue) × 365
  - Lower is better (faster cash collection)

## Red Flags to Watch For
- Declining margins over multiple quarters
- Revenue growth without corresponding cash flow growth
- Rising debt levels without clear ROI
- Deteriorating working capital
- Negative free cash flow for extended periods
- Increasing DSO (slower collections)
- Inventory building up faster than sales
