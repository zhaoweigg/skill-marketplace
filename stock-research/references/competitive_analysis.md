# Competitive Analysis Framework

## Porter's Five Forces Analysis

### 1. Competitive Rivalry
**Assess intensity of competition in the industry:**
- Number and size of competitors
- Industry growth rate (slow growth = more intense rivalry)
- Product differentiation (commoditized = more intense)
- Exit barriers (high = companies stay and compete)
- Fixed costs structure (high = price wars)

**Key Questions**:
- Who are the main competitors?
- What is market share distribution?
- Is competition intensifying or stabilizing?
- How do competitors differentiate?

### 2. Threat of New Entrants
**Barriers to entry:**
- Capital requirements
- Economies of scale
- Brand loyalty and switching costs
- Access to distribution channels
- Regulatory requirements
- Technology/patents
- Network effects

**Key Questions**:
- How easy is it for new players to enter?
- Has the company erected strong barriers?
- Are there recent new entrants gaining share?

### 3. Bargaining Power of Suppliers
**Supplier power indicators:**
- Number of suppliers (few = high power)
- Uniqueness of inputs
- Switching costs
- Supplier concentration vs. industry concentration
- Forward integration potential

**Key Questions**:
- Can suppliers raise prices easily?
- Is the company dependent on few suppliers?
- Does the company have alternatives?

### 4. Bargaining Power of Buyers
**Buyer power indicators:**
- Buyer concentration
- Volume of purchases
- Standardization of products
- Switching costs for buyers
- Backward integration potential
- Price sensitivity

**Key Questions**:
- Can customers easily switch to competitors?
- Do a few large customers dominate sales?
- Is the company a price-taker or price-maker?

### 5. Threat of Substitutes
**Substitute threat assessment:**
- Availability of alternative products/services
- Relative price-performance
- Switching costs
- Customer propensity to substitute

**Key Questions**:
- What alternatives exist to this product/service?
- Are substitutes becoming more competitive?
- How loyal are customers to this solution?

## Competitive Advantage (Moat) Assessment

### Types of Competitive Moats

**1. Cost Advantages**
- Economies of scale
- Proprietary technology/processes
- Favorable access to raw materials
- Geographic advantages
- Example: Walmart's distribution efficiency

**2. Network Effects**
- Product becomes more valuable as more people use it
- Creates high switching costs
- Examples: Facebook, Microsoft Office, Visa/Mastercard

**3. Intangible Assets**
- Brand strength and recognition
- Patents and intellectual property
- Regulatory licenses
- Examples: Coca-Cola brand, pharmaceutical patents

**4. Switching Costs**
- High cost/inconvenience to switch to competitor
- Data/ecosystem lock-in
- Integration complexity
- Examples: Enterprise software (SAP, Salesforce), Apple ecosystem

**5. Efficient Scale**
- Market can only support limited number of competitors
- Often in regulated or niche markets
- Examples: Utilities, regional newspapers (historically)

### Moat Assessment Questions
- Does the company have a sustainable competitive advantage?
- How wide is the moat? (Narrow/Moderate/Wide)
- Is the moat strengthening or eroding?
- Can competitors easily replicate the advantage?
- How long can the company maintain premium pricing or market share?

## Market Position Analysis

### Market Share
- Current market share percentage
- Trend over past 3-5 years (gaining or losing?)
- Relationship between market leader and followers
- Fragmented vs. concentrated market

### Competitive Positioning
- **Market Leader**: Usually has pricing power, sets trends
- **Challenger**: Growing fast, attacking leader
- **Follower**: Copies leader, competes on price
- **Nicher**: Focuses on specific segment

### Differentiation Analysis
How does the company stand out?
- Product quality/features
- Customer service
- Brand reputation
- Price positioning (premium, value, discount)
- Innovation/technology leadership
- Distribution advantages

## Industry Structure and Trends

### Industry Life Cycle
- **Emerging**: High growth, uncertain standards, many entrants
- **Growth**: Rapid expansion, some consolidation begins
- **Mature**: Slower growth, established players, efficiency focus
- **Decline**: Negative growth, consolidation, exit of players

### Industry Trends to Monitor
- Total addressable market (TAM) growth
- Technological disruption risks
- Regulatory changes
- Shift in customer preferences
- Consolidation or fragmentation trends
- Margin pressures or expansion

## Competitive Intelligence Checklist

**Company vs. Top Competitors**:
1. Revenue and growth rates
2. Profit margins (gross, operating, net)
3. Market share trends
4. Product/service portfolio breadth
5. Geographic presence
6. Customer segments served
7. Pricing strategy
8. Innovation track record
9. Financial health (debt, cash)
10. Management quality and track record

**Key Questions**:
- Why would customers choose this company over competitors?
- What could cause customers to switch away?
- Where is the company most vulnerable?
- What are its unique strengths?

## Red Flags
- Losing market share for multiple quarters
- Margins declining faster than peers
- High customer churn rates
- Inability to pass on cost increases
- Technological disruption gaining traction
- Commoditization of core products
- New entrants with innovative business models
- Weakening brand perception
